Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection. Common symptoms include high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, nausea, vomiting, swollen glands, and rash.

Influenza (flu) is a viral infection that attacks the respiratory system. Symptoms include fever, chills, muscle aches, cough, congestion, runny nose, headaches, and fatigue.

Malaria is a life-threatening disease transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes. Symptoms include fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea, vomiting, and muscle pain.

Typhoid fever is caused by Salmonella bacteria. Symptoms include prolonged fever, weakness, stomach pain, headache, loss of appetite, and sometimes rash.

COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. Symptoms include fever, cough, tiredness, loss of taste or smell, sore throat, and difficulty breathing in severe cases.
